# Debrief Template

Copy this template for each conversation debrief. Fill in every section - write "Nothing new" if a section is empty.

---

## Debrief: [Person] - [Role] - [Company] - [Date]

**Conversation type:** [Investor pitch / Customer discovery / Partner meeting / Advisor call / Other]
**Duration:** [approximate]
**Setting:** [In-person / Video / Phone / Async]

---

### 1. What Resonated

Capture exact phrases, metaphors, or framings that made them engage. Use their words, not yours.

| What You Said | Their Reaction | Persona | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| "[your exact phrase]" | [nodded / said "interesting" / asked follow-up / wrote it down] | [VC / Customer / Partner / etc.] | [what you were discussing] |

**Guidance:**
- Prioritize moments where they repeated your language back to you - that means it stuck
- Note body language if in-person (leaned in, started taking notes)
- If they shared your framing with someone else in the room, that's a strong signal
- Tag the persona type so you can reuse this language with similar audiences

---

### 2. What Confused Them

Moments where they asked for clarification, looked lost, or you had to re-explain.

| What Confused Them | Root Cause | Better Framing Found? |
|---|---|---|
| [topic or statement] | [jargon / wrong abstraction level / missing context / assumed knowledge] | [yes: "..." / no - needs work] |

**Guidance:**
- One person's confusion predicts many others' confusion
- Look for the root cause, not just the symptom. "They didn't get it" is not enough - why didn't they get it?
- If you found a better framing in the moment, capture both the bad and good version
- Jargon problems are different from abstraction problems. Jargon = wrong words. Abstraction = right words, wrong level.

---

### 3. What They Pushed Back On

Direct disagreements, skepticism, "yeah but" moments, or raised eyebrows.

| Their Objection (exact words) | Your Response | Did It Land? | Better Answer Needed? |
|---|---|---|---|
| "[their words]" | [what you said] | [yes / no / partially] | [yes: notes / no] |

**Guidance:**
- Capture their exact words, not your interpretation of their objection
- Be honest about whether your response landed. "Partially" is valid.
- If the same objection comes up 3+ times from different people, it's a positioning problem, not a one-off
- Note if the objection came from ignorance (they didn't know something) vs. disagreement (they knew and disagreed)

---

### 4. What They Asked That You Couldn't Answer

Questions where you stumbled, deflected, or needed to follow up.

| Their Question | Your Response | Answer Needed By |
|---|---|---|
| "[their question]" | [what you said / "deflected" / "said I'd follow up"] | [next pitch / this week / before next board meeting] |

**Guidance:**
- These questions tell you what smart people want to know that you haven't figured out yet
- "Good question" is a tell that you don't have the answer
- Prioritize by how often this question type will recur. A question every VC asks needs an answer before your next pitch. A one-off question from a domain expert can wait.
- If you deflected well, note the deflection technique so you can reuse it while you build the real answer

---

### 5. Positioning Drift Check

Did they understand what you're building? Did they compare you to the wrong thing?

| They Thought You Were | You're Actually | Correction Attempted | Did It Work? |
|---|---|---|---|
| "[their comparison or category]" | [correct positioning] | [what you said / nothing] | [yes / no / partially] |

**Guidance:**
- Misclassification is the #1 positioning problem for novel products
- Track WHICH wrong category they put you in. Patterns reveal what your market defaults to.
- If 3+ people make the same wrong comparison, your positioning has a leak
- A correction that works is gold. Capture the exact phrase.
- Common misclassifications: "So you're like [competitor] but..." / "Isn't that what [tool] does?" / "That sounds like [category]"

---

### 6. Next Step Agreed

Concrete commitments, not vague "let's stay in touch."

| Action | Owner | Timeline | Trigger/Dependency |
|---|---|---|---|
| [specific action] | [you / them / mutual] | [date or timeframe] | [what needs to happen first] |

**Guidance:**
- "Let's stay in touch" is not a next step. Push for specifics.
- If they committed to an intro, get the name and timeline
- If you committed to sending something, note exactly what and when
- "Warm" commitments with no timeline decay fast. Note which ones need a follow-up nudge.

---

### 7. Changes to Positioning

Things the conversation revealed about your current positioning that need updating.

| What Needs Changing | Why | Files Affected | Urgency |
|---|---|---|---|
| [specific claim, number, framing] | [what the conversation revealed] | [which canonical files] | [before next pitch / this week / when convenient] |

**Guidance:**
- Numbers that turned out to be wrong or unverifiable need immediate fixing
- Claims that got challenged and you couldn't defend need softening or proof
- Framings that consistently confuse need replacing
- If you're about to pitch someone similar, these changes are urgent

---

### 8. Proof Gaps Exposed

Moments where you needed data, a case study, a testimonial, or a reference and didn't have it.

| Proof Needed | When It Came Up | Potential Source |
|---|---|---|
| [type of evidence] | [context in conversation] | [where you might get it] |

**Guidance:**
- Proof gaps compound. Track them so you can fill them systematically.
- Types of proof: customer testimonials, usage metrics, case studies, third-party validation, analyst quotes, competitive benchmarks
- If a proof gap killed momentum in the conversation, that's high priority
- Note which persona type needs which proof. VCs need different evidence than customers.

---

## Post-Debrief Checklist

- [ ] All 8 sections filled (or marked "Nothing new")
- [ ] Exact phrases used (not paraphrases)
- [ ] Every insight tagged with persona type
- [ ] Next steps have owners and timelines
- [ ] Insights routed to correct canonical files
- [ ] No duplicates with existing canonical content
- [ ] Uncertain claims flagged with {{NEEDS_VALIDATION}}
- [ ] Relationship record updated with this conversation
